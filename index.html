He hasn't touched this account in years.

But tonight, something in the quiet pulls him back.

Martha's pie. The recipe—her recipe—must be here somewhere.

He opens the login page.

Select a password to begin.

1981. The year they said "yes" to everything—

the new apartment, the dog, the forever.

He can almost feel the confetti again.

The blue house by the lake.

She wanted it to feel like sky.

The walls still hold their arguments—

where the shelves should go, which curtains to keep.

The dog was hers first, then theirs.

Daisy never learned to fetch, only to follow.

He wonders if memory works the same way—

always running ahead, waiting for him to catch up.

Martha was simple at heart.

And the password… she would have made it simply: “Password.”

The message opens—blank, except for one line:

"You'll remember it when you need to."

He leans back. The smell of cinnamon and apples fills the room.

He smiles, realizing that he already does.
